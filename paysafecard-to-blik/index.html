Paysafecard to BLIK — FAQ

What is BLIK and how does it work?

BLIK is a Polish mobile payment standard operated by Polski Standard Płatności (PSP). It generates a 6-digit code in your bank app that authorises payments at merchants, ATMs, and online stores. BLIK is the most widely used contactless payment method in Poland.

Do I need a Polish bank account for BLIK?

Yes. BLIK is tied to Polish bank accounts only. If you have a Polish bank account at a participating bank, your BLIK code is available directly in that bank's mobile app.

Which Polish banks support BLIK?

PKO BP, mBank, Santander, ING, Alior Bank, BNP Paribas, Pekao, Bank Millennium, Credit Agricole, Getin Noble Bank, BOŚ Bank, and most Polish retail banks. Check blik.com for the complete list.

What currency is BLIK in?

BLIK operates exclusively in Polish Zloty (PLN). Note: use a PLN-denominated Paysafecard for best conversion rates.
