Paysafecard to Monero (XMR) — FAQ

Is Monero truly private — not just "more private" than Bitcoin?

Yes. Monero uses ring signatures (to obfuscate the sender), stealth addresses (to hide the receiver), and RingCT (to conceal the amount) on every single transaction by default. Unlike Bitcoin's optional privacy tools, Monero's privacy is mandatory and applies equally to every transaction.

What wallet should I use for Monero?

The official Monero GUI Wallet from getmonero.org offers maximum security and features. Feather Wallet is a lightweight desktop alternative. Cake Wallet is the best option for iOS and Android. For cold storage, Trezor Model T and Ledger (via Monero CLI) support XMR.

Why do Monero transactions take longer to confirm?

Monero requires 10 network confirmations before funds are spendable — approximately 20–30 minutes. This is intentional: it prevents transaction graph analysis attacks and ensures privacy is preserved even for newly received funds.

Is Monero legal?

Monero is legal to buy and hold in most countries including UK, Germany, France, and most EU member states. Some exchanges have voluntarily delisted XMR due to regulatory compliance preferences, but ownership is not prohibited.

Monero Privacy Best Practices

Use subaddresses for every incoming transaction

Monero's subaddress feature generates unique receiving addresses from a single wallet seed. Using a different subaddress for each payment source prevents anyone from linking your incoming transactions together.

Sync your wallet completely before checking balance

Monero wallets must scan the entire blockchain to identify your transactions. Allow 10–30 minutes for a full sync when first opening the wallet. Your balance will be 0 during sync — this is normal.

Wait for 10 confirmations before spending

Monero funds are locked for 10 confirmations (~20 min) after receipt. This is a network rule designed to preserve privacy and prevent attacks. Do not attempt transactions on unconfirmed incoming funds.
